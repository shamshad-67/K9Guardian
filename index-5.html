The Build

Not a leash accessory.
A piece of working gear.

K9Nova was built the way working-dog equipment is built — for load, for grip, and for the moments a soft-goods leash simply can't handle.

The shell is cut from 1680D ballistic nylon, the same weight class used in tactical packs, then backed with a breathable mesh core so it stays comfortable across a full day on trail. The front-clip design redirects forward pull at the chest instead of the throat, so a dog that lunges learns to walk beside you, not ahead of you.

Every stitch line is reflective. Every strap is rated. Every buckle is built to be opened with one hand in the rain.

Why K9Nova

We build gear like your dog's safety depends on it. Because it does.

K9Nova started with a simple frustration: most dog gear is designed to look good in a product photo, not to survive a real life. Trail dust, rain, a sudden lunge at a squirrel, three hundred walks a year — ordinary harnesses weren't built for any of it.

So we went to the source. We studied the materials used in working and service dog equipment, the kind built for dogs that can't afford gear failure, and brought that same standard to a harness anyone could buy. Mil-spec nylon. Reinforced stress points. A fit system that actually adjusts to your dog, not just the size chart.

Every K9Nova harness is tested the way it'll actually be used — on trails, in rain, against dogs that pull harder than they should. If it can't hold up there, it doesn't ship.
